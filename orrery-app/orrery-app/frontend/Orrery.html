Understanding the Orrery

The Orrery serves as the core of our application, functioning as the primary entry point for visualizing the motions of the planets in our solar system. It inherits from the JavaFX Application class, which allows for a rich graphical user interface.

The start method is called during the graphical initialization process. This method sets up the main Stage object and builds the application layout through the buildLayout method. The Orrery object manages the window's contents, encapsulating both the Space and Info classes, which represent the moving planets and the information panel, respectively.

Initially, the Orrery class directly managed the Planet class. However, as the interface evolved, the responsibilities became blurred. Consequently, the planets were separated into the Space class, focusing solely on their animation, while the Info class handles the display of planetary data. This separation allows for a clearer structure and better management of the application's components.

What is an Orrery?

An orrery is an apparatus that demonstrates the relative positions and motions of celestial bodies in the solar system, typically represented by balls that are moved by a clockwork mechanism. In our digital version, these movements are simulated to provide an interactive experience for users, allowing them to explore the dynamics of planetary motion in an engaging way.
