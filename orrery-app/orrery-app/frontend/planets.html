The Planets

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in our solar system. It has a very thin atmosphere, allowing for extreme temperature variations.

Orbital Period: 88 days

Mass: 0.055 Earths

Venus

Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in our solar system.

Orbital Period: 225 days

Mass: 0.815 Earths

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life. It has a diverse climate and is covered by 71% water.

Orbital Period: 365 days

Mass: 1 Earth

Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet due to its iron oxide-rich soil. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system.

Orbital Period: 687 days

Mass: 0.107 Earths

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, known for its Great Red Spot and strong magnetic field.

Orbital Period: 11.86 years

Mass: 317.8 Earths

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning rings made of ice and rock particles. It has the second-largest mass in the solar system.

Orbital Period: 29.46 years

Mass: 95.2 Earths

Uranus

Uranus is unique for its sideways rotation and is known as an "ice giant" due to the presence of water, ammonia, and methane in its atmosphere.

Orbital Period: 84 years

Mass: 14.5 Earths

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is known for its striking blue color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere.

Orbital Period: 165 years

Mass: 17.1 Earths
